The Problem

Product teams fail before
engineering begins.

  • Most execution breakdowns trace back to a decision that was never clearly made — scope was assumed, constraints were implicit, ownership was fuzzy
  • By the time engineers start building, the structural gaps are already locked in — rework, misalignment, and re-scoping become inevitable
  • The tools teams use — Jira, Notion, Linear — track work in progress. None of them structure the decision before the work starts

The Cost

Ambiguity is expensive.
The bill arrives late.

  • PM time lost to rework: PMs spend an estimated 30–40% of their time re-clarifying scope, re-aligning stakeholders, and rewriting requirements that should have been locked before kickoff
  • Post-sprint fixes cost more: Defects and scope gaps caught after engineering begins cost 5–10× more to resolve than those caught at decision time — a well-documented pattern in software delivery
  • Feature waste compounds: Industry research consistently shows that a significant portion of shipped features see minimal or no sustained use — the root cause is often unclear success criteria, not poor engineering

The tax isn't visible in the sprint — it shows up in the post-mortem.

The Insight

Execution problems are often
decision problems upstream.

  • The signals are structural, not individual — the same gaps recur across teams, orgs, and initiatives: unclear inputs, unstated assumptions, missing constraints, undefined success
  • These aren't PM failures. They're system failures — the product stack has no designated layer for decision readiness before commitment
  • When ambiguity is structural, the fix must be structural too — not a better template, but a purpose-built layer that sits at the decision moment

Differentiation

Not another AI doc tool.
